Traditional payment rails begin to break down in agentic systems due to three key limitations.

First, there is no native concept of conditional execution. Payment systems are designed to transfer funds, not to enforce whether specific execution-related conditions are actually met at the time of payment.

Second, payments are not based on verified outcomes. Instead, they follow a pay-before or pay-after model, where the system assumes that the task will be completed correctly rather than validating it.

Third, there is no built-in verification layer. Existing systems do not provide a way to link execution, payment, and delivery into a single verifiable process, which makes it difficult to ensure correctness and accountability.

Together, these limitations show that traditional payment rails are built to move money securely, but not to ensure that money is transferred based on actual execution.
